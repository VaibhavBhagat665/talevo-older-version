Space Explorer

Chapter 1: The Discovery

The abandoned space station loomed ahead, its angular silhouette stark against the backdrop of swirling cosmic dust. Captain Alex Zhang checked the radiation monitors one last time before giving the nod to his second-in-command.

"All readings normal," Lieutenant Kai reported, her eyes flicking between holographic displays. "But there's something weird about the energy signature. It's... pulsing."

Alex frowned. This wasn't in the mission briefing. The derelict station—Outpost Epsilon—had been abandoned for decades after the Resource Wars. It should be dead, powerless. Yet here it was, somehow active.

"Could be automated systems," he suggested, though he didn't believe it himself. "Or scavengers."

Kai shook her head. "No registered salvage operations in this sector. And this signal..." She manipulated the display, zooming in on a waveform pattern. "I've never seen anything like it."

The ship's AI chimed in, its voice calm and measured. "Analysis indicates the energy signature contains embedded data patterns consistent with communication attempts."

A chill ran down Alex's spine. "Communication? From whom?"

"Unknown," the AI replied. "Pattern does not match any known human or allied species transmission protocols."

The crew fell silent. First contact wasn't part of their mission parameters. They were here to salvage technology, not make history—or possibly get themselves killed by an unknown entity.

Alex studied the station through the viewscreen. Their mission was clear: dock, retrieve valuable tech, and return to Earth. Simple. Safe. Profitable.

But that was before they detected signs of intelligent activity.

"Captain," Kai said quietly. "We need to make a decision."

What will you do?
